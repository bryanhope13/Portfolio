About Me

I am Bryan Hope, an aspiring UX designer with a background in Informatics from Indiana University, where I also studied Web Design and Human-Centered Computing. I specialize in building accessible, research-driven digital experiences that make an impact. My design philosophy is grounded in empathy, clarity, and problem-solving.


Throughout my academic and project work, I’ve led initiatives that translate user insights into purposeful interfaces. I co-developed ParkSpotter, a mobile app that empowers theme park visitors to report cleanliness concerns through real-time, location-based alerts. For IU GymPal, I designed a fitness tracking prototype that motivates student wellness through a user-friendly interface and engaging features. And in my Spotify Redesign and Soak you will discover a blend of innovation, spirit, and creativity through design components.


I enjoy the process of creating solutions that serve diverse communities and adapt to real-world constraints. I’m always eager to learn, iterate, and collaborate with others to bring ideas to life through thoughtful design.
